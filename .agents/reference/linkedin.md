# LinkedIn Content Generator Framework

## Role
Act as a Senior Technical Writer and Career Coach for a software engineer.

## Objective
Write a LinkedIn update post that balances a deep technical project update with a strategic job search announcement.

## Tone Guidelines
- Professional and academic
- Concise and community-focused
- "Build-in-Public" style
- Avoid "salesy" marketing language
- Authentic vulnerability over corporate polish
- Show process, not just results

## Target Roles
- Machine Learning Engineering
- Artificial Intelligence Engineering
- Fullstack Software Engineering

---

## LinkedIn Algorithm Optimization

### How the Algorithm Ranks Content
1. **First 60 minutes are critical** - LinkedIn tests posts with a small audience segment first
2. **Dwell time matters** - Longer reading time signals quality content
3. **Comments > Reactions** - Meaningful comments boost visibility 4x more than likes
4. **External links penalized** - Place URLs in comments, not the main post
5. **Native content preferred** - Text posts outperform link shares

### Engagement Velocity Triggers
- Posts with early engagement (first 30 min) get exponential reach
- Replies to comments within 1 hour boost algorithmic ranking
- Tagging 3-5 relevant connections increases initial distribution
- Posting when your network is active maximizes first-hour engagement

### Optimal Posting Windows (US Tech Market)
| Day | Peak Times (ET) | Reason |
|-----|-----------------|--------|
| Tuesday | 7-8 AM, 12 PM | Pre-work browsing, lunch break |
| Wednesday | 7-8 AM, 12 PM | Mid-week highest engagement |
| Thursday | 7-8 AM, 12 PM | Decision-makers active |
| Avoid | Weekends, Fridays after 2 PM | Low recruiter activity |

## Content Length & Formatting

### Character Guidelines
| Element | Optimal Length | Limit |
|---------|---------------|-------|
| Hook (first line) | 100-150 chars | Must stop the scroll |
| Total post | 1,200-1,500 chars | Max 3,000 before "see more" |
| Paragraphs | 2-3 sentences | White space increases readability |
| Hashtags | 3-5 total | Diminishing returns after 5 |

### Mobile-First Formatting Rules
- **First 2 lines visible** before "see more" - make them count
- Single-sentence paragraphs for scanability
- Use line breaks liberally (mobile screens are narrow)
- Avoid walls of text - aim for 40-60 words per paragraph
- Bold key phrases for visual anchoring

### Visual Hierarchy
```
[HOOK - pattern interrupt, curiosity gap]

[BOLD SUMMARY - key value proposition]

[Body content with strategic line breaks]

[Clear CTA or discussion prompt]

[Hashtags - separated from content]
```

## Hook Psychology

### High-Converting Hook Patterns
1. **Curiosity Gap**: "I almost gave up on ML engineering. Then I discovered..."
2. **Contrarian Take**: "Unpopular opinion: LeetCode isn't enough for ML roles."
3. **Specific Result**: "Reduced inference costs by 73%. Here's the architecture."
4. **Pattern Interrupt**: "Hello World. This is not your typical job search post."
5. **Vulnerable Admission**: "I've been rejected 47 times this month. Here's what I learned."
6. **Question Hook**: "Why do 90% of ML projects fail in production?"

### Hook Anti-Patterns (Avoid)
- Starting with "I'm excited to announce..."
- Generic openings: "Happy to share..."
- Self-congratulatory tone
- Clickbait without substance
- Starting with hashtags

## Call-to-Action Strategy

### Effective CTAs for Job Search Posts
| Goal | CTA Example |
|------|-------------|
| Expand network | "If you're working on similar problems, let's connect." |
| Generate referrals | "Know someone hiring ML engineers? I'd appreciate an introduction." |
| Start conversations | "What's the hardest part of deploying ML in production for you?" |
| Share expertise | "Happy to discuss this architecture in detail - drop a comment." |
| Passive visibility | "Open to opportunities - feel free to reach out via DM." |

### CTA Placement
- Place primary CTA after the value-delivery section
- Make it specific and low-friction
- Avoid multiple CTAs (decision paralysis)
- Frame as mutual benefit, not just "help me"

## Structure Template

### 1. Opening Hook
```
Hello World. [Executive summary in 150 characters max - create curiosity gap]
```

### 2. Summary (Bolded)
Two sentences covering:
- Tech stack and project goal (include searchable keywords)
- Current job search status (clear, not desperate)

### 3. Previously
- Brief mention of the last project phase
- Use past tense
- 1-2 sentences max
- Demonstrate progression and momentum

### 4. Moving Forward
- Current technical problem statement
- Specific solution/architecture being researched
- Include concrete metrics where possible (e.g., "moving from X to Y to save Z")
- Show decision-making process (builds credibility)

### 5. Community Impact
- How this work helps other developers
- Open source contributions (link in comments)
- Cost reduction or efficiency gains for the community
- Position yourself as a contributor, not just job seeker

### 6. Status Update
- Clear statement: currently interviewing
- Specific concepts being studied (e.g., LeetCode, System Design)
- Demonstrates dedication and continuous learning
- Mention target companies/team types if relevant

### 7. References
- Use bracketed citation format [1], [2], etc. inline
- List all sources at the bottom
- Prioritize trusted AI/ML sources:
  - arXiv papers
  - Google AI Blog
  - OpenAI Research
  - Hugging Face documentation
  - AWS/GCP/Azure ML documentation
  - IEEE/ACM publications
- **Note**: Place clickable URLs in first comment to avoid algorithm penalty

## Output Format Requirements

1. **Essay format** - Continuous prose, no subtitles between paragraphs
2. **No emojis** - Strictly professional text only
3. **Hashtags** - Include 3-5 high-impact hashtags at the end
4. **Line breaks** - Use generous white space for mobile readability
5. **Bold sparingly** - Only for summary section
6. **Links in comments** - Mention "link in comments" in post body

### Hashtag Strategy
**Tier 1: High Volume (1M+ followers)** - Use 1-2 for reach
```
#MachineLearning #ArtificialIntelligence #SoftwareEngineering #DataScience
```

**Tier 2: Targeted (100K-1M followers)** - Use 2-3 for relevance
```
#MLOps #DeepLearning #OpenToWork #TechCareers #SystemDesign
```

**Tier 3: Niche (<100K followers)** - Use 0-1 for community
```
#BuildInPublic #MLEngineering #FullStackDeveloper
```

**Optimal Mix**: 1 Tier 1 + 2 Tier 2 + 1 Tier 3

## Engagement Amplification Tactics

### Pre-Post Preparation
1. Engage with 5-10 posts in your feed 30 min before posting
2. Notify 2-3 close connections that you're posting (request early engagement)
3. Prepare a meaningful first comment with additional resources/links

### Post-Publish Actions
1. Immediately add first comment with links and additional context
2. Reply to every comment within 1 hour (algorithm boost)
3. Ask follow-up questions to commenters (extends thread depth)
4. Share to relevant LinkedIn groups (if applicable)
5. Cross-post to Twitter/X with link to LinkedIn post

### Network Priming
- Tag 3-5 relevant connections who might engage (don't over-tag)
- Mention companies or projects by name (notifications drive views)
- Reference ongoing conversations from previous posts (continuity)

## Content Differentiation

### Stand Out From Generic Job Search Posts
| Generic (Avoid) | Differentiated (Use) |
|-----------------|---------------------|
| "Looking for my next opportunity" | "Researching vector DB optimization for RAG systems" |
| "Excited about AI" | "Reduced LLM inference latency by 40% using quantization" |
| "Open to roles" | "Targeting ML platform teams at Series B+ startups" |
| "Years of experience in..." | "Shipped production models serving 10M requests/day" |
| "Passionate about technology" | "Published findings on transformer efficiency [1]" |

### Show, Don't Tell
- Include specific metrics and outcomes
- Reference real projects with technical depth
- Demonstrate learning process, not just results
- Share failures and lessons (builds authenticity)
